By the end, you will be able to:

01

Speak the language of private markets

Carry, capital calls, DPI, vintages, GP and LP — the words that make this world feel closed. You’ll use them comfortably in any investor room, because you’ll know what they mean and why they matter.

02

Find funds that match your values

A guided system that translates what you care about — climate, health, equity, community, and more — into the fund strategies that put money behind it, plus how to confirm a fund lives its claims.

03

Evaluate any fund like an LP

A detailed breakdown of how to evaluate a fund — the team, the strategy, the results, the fees, and the operations — in the order experienced investors look at them, with the exact questions to ask.

04

Know what amount makes sense for you

A clear formula that turns your net worth, timeline, and comfort level into a specific dollar range — so if and when you decide to invest, the number is yours, not someone else’s suggestion.

05

Test yourself on a practice fund

Your capstone is a complete practice fund case, built just for this program. You’ll run a full fund evaluation from start to finish and see exactly how much you’ve learned — before any real money is ever involved.

06

Know what happens after you invest

The first 90 days mapped out: what arrives in your inbox, what to do with it, when money moves, how taxes work, and the rhythm of the years that follow. No surprises, no guessing.

Success isn’t writing a check. Success is knowing how venture capital works, what funds fit your values-aligned investment thesis, and how to decide when to invest. Some people finish this program and invest within the year. Others decide private markets aren’t right for them yet. Both leave with the same thing: a decision made with confidence.

Four phases. Six modules. One decision system.

Phase 1

Know yourself as an investor

Your money story, your liquidity map, your honest baseline. Capital follows identity so this program starts with yours.

Phase 2

Learn the asset class

Private markets and venture, in plain language. Power law, fee math, and the three routes in — fund, angel, SPV — compared on your inputs.

Phase 3

Decide like an LP

Build your investment thesis, run real diligence on the practice fund, size what an investment would look like for you, and earn your graduate credential.

Phase 4

Maintain the momentum

Invest now, invest later, or keep learning — all three are wins. When you’re ready, the first-90-days roadmap, LP community membership, and quarterly practice are waiting.

One common question

Do I need to be accredited to participate?

No. You need to be accredited to invest in most funds — not to learn. This program is the right fit for new LP's, recently accredited, or soon-to-be accredited investors who want to learn how to invest in the private markets.
